The Process

Four hours. One thousand years.

Every Monolith brick passes through the same four disciplines a Roman kiln-master would recognise — slowed down, scrutinised, and refused at the first sign of flaw.

01

Clay

Reddish alluvial clay, aged six months in open air so the minerals settle and the grain tightens.

02

Shape

Hand-struck into oiled oak moulds — never pressed by machine, so each face keeps a faint, deliberate imperfection.

03

Fire

72 hours in a wood-fed kiln at 1,150°C. The colour you see is chemistry, not paint.

04

Cure

Rested for three weeks in cedar racks before a single unit is approved for sale.
